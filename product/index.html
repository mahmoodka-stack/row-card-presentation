Product opportunity

Why this product matters

The BRD positions this as a new product line for RoW customers: a standalone USD IBAN, SWIFT-only, with an attached USD Visa debit card and a virtual-first launch model.

The strongest product story is not "banking infrastructure." It is a simple, global USD account experience that removes funding friction and gives customers a spendable balance from day one.
Product goals

  • Standardize funding

    Give RoW customers one clear USD funding route instead of fragmented local rails.

  • Make the balance usable

    Link the IBAN to a card so the customer can spend without extra steps.

  • Keep launch scope controlled

    Avoid a broad rollout before the operating model is proven.

Phase 1 scope

What is in and out

Phase 1 is intentionally narrow: USD only, SWIFT only, inbound MT103 only, virtual card mandatory, no automatic FX, and no outbound SWIFT withdrawals at launch.

USD only SWIFT only Inbound MT103 Virtual-first No FX
Included vs excluded

  • Included

    USD IBAN, inbound SWIFT, direct ledger funding, virtual card, card spend on the same ledger.

  • Excluded

    Outbound SWIFT, automatic FX, SEPA, Faster Payments, and a separate wallet in Phase 1.

  • Future phase

    Physical card expansion, outbound withdrawals, and any broader rail support.

Customer journey

How the product works for the user

The product experience should feel straightforward: the customer receives a USD IBAN, sends money in via SWIFT, sees the balance credited after settlement, and then spends from the same ledger.

This is a key product principle: keep the journey understandable. The product should feel like one USD account with one clear purpose, not a complex banking setup.

Product decisions

What the team should align on

The BRD still leaves a few product decisions open. These are the ones worth resolving in the product meeting because they affect naming, customer understanding, and launch readiness.

1. Naming How should the USD IBAN be presented to customers so it is clearly different from any existing USD trading or funding balance?
2. Eligibility Should virtual card be mandatory for everyone, with physical card eligibility controlled by tier or subscription later?
3. First-party funding rule How explicit should the Me-to-Me rule be in product copy, onboarding, and support flows?
4. Customer promise Do we sell this as a payment account, a banking account, or a spendable USD balance with a card?
Product risks

What could confuse customers

The BRD calls out the main friction points: non-USD inbound handling, SWIFT fees, transfer delays, difference between IBAN funding and trading funding, and the lack of outbound from the IBAN in Phase 1.

Non-USD rejected SWIFT not instant Correspondent fees No outbound in Phase 1 Me-to-Me only
Product guardrails

  • Make the balance naming obvious

    The BRD highlights a high risk of confusion between IBAN and trading balances.

  • Set fee expectations

    Customers may send one amount and receive less if correspondent fees are taken in transit.

  • Explain delays up front

    SWIFT is not instant, so the onboarding and support message needs to say that clearly.

  • Avoid launch ambiguity

    If the product story is too broad, support and risk teams will inherit the confusion.
