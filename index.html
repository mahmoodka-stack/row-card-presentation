Executive view

What the product is solving

The product creates a standalone USD SWIFT IBAN for RoW customers, integrated via Tribe and powered by OpenPayd, with an attached USD Visa card and virtual-first launch.

Phase 1 is intentionally narrow: inbound SWIFT only, USD only, no FX conversion, and no client-initiated SWIFT withdrawals at launch. That keeps delivery focused while creating a foundation for later expansion.
Three messages for leadership

  • Clear positioning

    This is a new USD payment account, separate from the existing EUR/SEPA experience.

  • Controlled launch

    Virtual-first, inbound SWIFT only, no outbound SWIFT, and no automatic FX.

  • Operational readiness

    Fee handling, reconciliation, AML monitoring, support scripts, and reporting all need alignment.

Product scope

Phase 1 scope and exclusions

The account model is USD-only and SWIFT-only. Phase 1 supports inbound MT103; outbound MT103, MT940/MT942, MT202, SWIFT Express, and non-SWIFT rails are out of scope.

USD only SWIFT only MT103 inbound Virtual card mandatory No separate wallet
Included vs excluded

  • Included

    USD IBAN, inbound SWIFT, virtual card, and direct ledger-based authorization.

  • Excluded

    Outbound SWIFT, automatic FX, SEPA, Faster Payments, and a separate wallet.

  • Future phase

    Outbound withdrawals and broader payment-rail support after controls are in place.

Payment flow

How money moves in Phase 1

The inbound flow is simple: client sends USD via SWIFT, OpenPayd receives the funds, Tribe gets the webhook, credits the USD balance, and the value sweeps to the master account.

Non-USD inbound should not be auto-converted. It should be rejected or held for manual handling, with a clear customer-facing process for returns and exceptions.

Stakeholder views

What each team needs to care about

The main themes are fee logic, webhook handling, reconciliation, AML monitoring, shipping readiness, and customer communications.

Ops & Risk

  • Non-USD handling

    Reject or return without auto-FX; define an escalation path.

  • Me-to-Me funding

    Sender-name matching, first-party only, and monitoring for suspicious patterns.

  • Card readiness

    Virtual by default, with physical issuance supported later.

Finance, BI & CS

  • Reconciliation

    Inbound, returns, fee effects, and settlement visibility need clear mapping.

  • Reporting

    Country, entity, card vs IBAN inflows, and instructed vs credited amounts.

  • Support messaging

    SWIFT delays, correspondent fees, and balance naming need clean scripts.

Delivery roadmap

How the project should be phased

The delivery model should move through detailed design, implementation, and go-live, with dependencies on OpenPayd documentation, sandbox parity, Visa readiness, Tribe resources, and compliance approval.
