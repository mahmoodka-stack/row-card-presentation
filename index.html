Executive view

What the product is solving

The BRD proposes a USD-only SWIFT IBAN for RoW customers, powered by OpenPayd and integrated via Tribe, with an attached USD Visa debit card and a virtual-first launch model. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Phase 1 is intentionally narrow: inbound SWIFT only, USD only, no FX conversion, and no client-initiated SWIFT withdrawals. That keeps launch scope focused while creating a foundation for later expansion. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7} :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
Three messages for leadership
1

Clear product positioning

New USD payment account, separate from existing EUR/SEPA capability.

2

3

Operational readiness required

Fee visibility, reconciliation, AML, support scripts, and country/risk handling.

Product scope

Phase 1 scope and exclusions

The account model is USD-only and SWIFT-only. Phase 1 supports inbound MT103; outbound MT103, MT940/MT942, MT202, SWIFT Express, and non-SWIFT rails are out of scope. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Included vs excluded

  • Included

    USD-only IBAN, inbound SWIFT, virtual card, direct ledger-based auth.

  • Excluded

    Outbound SWIFT, automatic FX, SEPA, Faster Payments, separate wallet.

  • Future phase

    Outbound withdrawals and broader payment scheme expansion.

Stakeholder views

What each team needs to care about

The BRD highlights impacts across Tribe, OpenPayd, eToro, Finance, Ops, Risk, BI, and Customer Support. The main themes are fee logic, webhook handling, reconciliation, AML monitoring, shipping readiness, and customer communications.

Ops & Risk

  • Non-USD handling

    Reject or return without auto-FX; define escalation path.

  • Me-to-Me funding

    Sender name matching, first-party only, monitoring for suspicious patterns.

  • Card readiness

    Virtual by default; physical capability should be future-proofed.

Finance, BI & CS

  • Reconciliation

    Inbound, returns, fee effects, and Visa settlement visibility.

  • Reporting

    Country, entity, card vs IBAN inflows, instructed vs credited amounts.

  • Support messaging

    SWIFT delays, correspondent fees, and balance naming need clear scripts.

Delivery roadmap

How the project should be phased

The BRD breaks delivery into detailed design, implementation, and go-live, with dependencies on OpenPayd documentation, sandbox parity, Visa readiness, Tribe resources, and compliance sign-off. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Phase A
Detailed design

Confirm MT types and fee logic

Finalize return handling, scheme fee treatment, and refined estimates.

Phase B
Build & UAT

Implement Tribe + OpenPayd configuration

Webhook parsing, ledger updates, sandbox work, and testing.

Phase C
Launch

Go live with monitoring

Production enablement, compliance approval, and launch monitoring.

Open questions

Topics to confirm before launch

The BRD still needs confirmations around correspondent fee visibility, exact MT103 fields captured by Tribe, return handling details, and how physical issuance and international shipping will be operationalized if needed later.

Pending decisions

  • Fee visibility

    Can Tribe/OpenPayd expose instructed vs net amount and correspondent deductions?

  • Non-USD inbound

    Auto-reject or hold and return manually?

  • Physical cards

    Which tiers, what shipping model, and what operational ownership?

  • Country/risk segmentation

    How should jurisdiction and sub-BIN logic be reported?
